# Periodicity of Elements

## 1. Fundamental Laws
* **Mendeleev's Periodic Law:** The physical and chemical properties of elements are a periodic function of their atomic masses. (Modern law uses atomic numbers).

## 2. Electronic Configuration Rules
To understand periodicity, we must understand how electrons fill atoms:

* **Pauli Exclusion Principle:** No two electrons in an atom can have the same set of four quantum numbers ($n, l, m_l, m_s$). An orbital holds max 2 electrons with opposite spins.
* **Aufbau Principle:** Electrons fill orbitals of lowest energy levels first ($1s \rightarrow 2s \rightarrow 2p \dots$).
* **Hund's Rule:** For degenerate orbitals (like $p, d, f$), electrons fill each orbital singly with parallel spins before pairing up.
* **Heisenberg Uncertainty Principle:** It is impossible to determine simultaneously both the exact position and momentum of an electron ($\Delta x \Delta p \geq \frac{h}{4\pi}$).

## 3. Periodic Trends
| Property | Definition | Trend Across Period ($\rightarrow$) | Trend Down Group ($\downarrow$) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Atomic Radius** | Distance from nucleus to outermost electron. | **Decreases** (Effective nuclear charge increases) | **Increases** (Number of shells increases) |
| **Ionization Energy** | Energy required to remove the outermost electron. | **Increases** (Harder to remove) | **Decreases** (Easier to remove) |
| **Electron Affinity** | Energy change when an atom gains an electron. | **Increases** (More negative/exothermic) | **Decreases** (Less attraction) |
